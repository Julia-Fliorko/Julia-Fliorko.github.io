Game Sales Analysis

This project analyzes historical video game sales to surface market structure, platform lifecycle dynamics, genre performance, and regional demand differences. The goal is to convert raw sales records into decision-ready insights for portfolio planning and market prioritization.

The entire workflow is executed in Excel, using structured cleaning, pivot-based analysis, and calculated metrics to create a consistent analytical narrative.

Project Overview

Business Questions

  • How are sales distributed across regions, and what does that imply for market focus?
  • Which platforms drive the most sales, and how does performance evolve over time?
  • Which genres lead globally, and where do regional preferences diverge?
  • How concentrated is publisher success, and what does market power look like?
  • What long-term trends are visible, and what structural factors may explain them?

Problem

Video game sales performance is influenced by region-specific demand, platform lifecycle stages, genre fit, and publisher market power. Without structured analysis, it is easy to misinterpret global aggregates, overestimate broad demand for a genre, or miss platform timing effects.

This project reframes sales history into actionable market signals by separating results across platform, genre, publisher, and region, while also tracking how these relationships change over time.

Dataset

Structure

The dataset contains sales records at the game level, including platform, genre, publisher, release year, and regional sales across North America, Europe, Japan, and Other, plus total Global Sales.

This structure supports segmentation, ranking, and cross-region comparison without requiring external joins.

Preparation

Data was standardized to support consistent aggregation and trend analysis. Records with missing or non-informative attributes were handled to avoid distortion in platform and regional comparisons.

Regional totals were validated against global values to ensure arithmetic consistency.

Approach

1) Data Cleaning and Standardization

Cleaned categorical fields (platform, genre, publisher) and normalized year and sales columns to enable reliable pivoting and comparisons.

2) Pivot-Based Market Segmentation

Built pivot views by region, platform, genre, and publisher to isolate performance drivers and prevent global aggregates from masking regional variance.

3) Ranking and Concentration Analysis

Ranked top platforms, genres, and publishers by sales and evaluated concentration effects to understand how much of the market is captured by a small set of leaders.

4) Trend and Lifecycle Interpretation

Compared year-over-year patterns to highlight platform lifecycle timing and long-term shifts in the sales landscape.

Key Findings

1) Regional Sales Concentration

Global sales are dominated by North America and Europe, which collectively account for most revenue. Japan is smaller in total volume but structurally distinct, with different genre and platform preferences relative to Western regions.

2) Platform Lifecycle Effects

Platform performance follows predictable lifecycle dynamics: sales tend to peak during periods of broad adoption and decline as platforms age. Strong genres do not fully offset late-lifecycle slowdowns.

3) Genre Performance is Region-Dependent

While mass-market genres tend to lead globally, regional segmentation reveals meaningful deviations: Japanâ€™s distribution differs from Western markets, indicating that genre strategy should be adapted per region rather than optimized only for global totals.

4) Publisher Market Power

Sales are highly concentrated among a small set of publishers. Leadership is sustained through repeated high-performing releases, while the long tail contributes marginally on an individual basis.

5) Long-Term Trend Requires Context

Total sales show a visible historical peak followed by decline. This pattern is consistent with market transitions and industry transformation rather than indicating a single-region demand shock.

Implications

  • Prioritize by region: global aggregates hide regional preference structure.
  • Time platform bets: lifecycle stage is a dominant factor in sales outcomes.
  • Localize genre strategy: genre leadership differs across markets.
  • Account for concentration: publisher success is skewed and persistent.
  • Interpret trends carefully: shifts often reflect structural transformation, not demand collapse.
