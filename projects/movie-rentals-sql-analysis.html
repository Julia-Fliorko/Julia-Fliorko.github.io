Movie Rentals SQL Analysis

This project uses SQL to identify revenue drivers and assess inventory utilization for a movie rental business. The goal is to support decisions around market focus, customer segments, and catalog optimization.

The workflow includes data exploration, query-based metrics, and stakeholder-ready reporting. Results are presented in an interactive Tableau dashboard.

Problem

The business needs clarity on what drives revenue and how effectively inventory is being used. This analysis focuses on customer demand patterns, market contribution, and catalog performance.

The goal is to translate rental activity into clear metrics that can support decisions about targeting, inventory planning, and customer strategy.

Dataset

Source

Relational movie rental database with tables covering customers, rentals, payments, inventory, films, categories, and store locations.

Analysis uses joins and aggregations to connect customer activity to revenue and inventory.

Notes

The work relies on consistent keys (customer, rental, inventory, film) and time fields to measure trends and utilization accurately.

Outputs are provided as tables for reuse in dashboards and reporting.

Approach

1) Data Familiarization

Reviewed schema and relationships, validated join paths, and identified fields needed for revenue and inventory metrics.

2) KPI Queries

Built SQL queries for customer counts, revenue contribution, rental volume, and time-based patterns (daily/hourly/weekday).

3) Inventory Utilization

Measured rental activity against inventory availability to highlight high-rotation titles and underutilized stock.

4) Reporting

Exported query outputs for Tableau and Excel and organized results into a dashboard for exploration.

Results

  • Market contribution: identifies where customers and revenue are concentrated by country.
  • Demand patterns: highlights rental peaks by day and hour to support operational planning.
  • Catalog performance: compares rentals across titles/categories to locate key drivers.
  • Utilization: flags inventory that is over-rotating or underused for optimization.
